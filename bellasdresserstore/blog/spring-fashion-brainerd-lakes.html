The Color Story of Spring 2025

This season's palette makes my French heart sing! We're seeing a beautiful marriage of soft, romantic hues with unexpected pops of joy. Think of it as Monet's garden meets Minnesota lake life.

This Season's Must-Have Colors

These colors aren't just beautiful - they're strategic! They complement the fresh green of new leaves, the blue of our lakes as the ice melts, and most importantly, they bring out that post-winter glow we're all craving.

Key Pieces in Our Spring Collection

1. The Garden Party Dress Collection

Delicate florals that would make the gardens of Giverny jealous! I've sourced dresses with prints ranging from tiny liberty-style flowers to bold botanical statements. Perfect for Easter brunch, spring weddings, or simply feeling beautiful on a Tuesday.

Styling tip: Pair with a denim jacket for those cool spring mornings, then remove for afternoon sunshine!

2. Transitional Midi Dresses

These are the workhorses of spring! In fabrics substantial enough for our unpredictable weather but light enough to feel fresh. Think cotton blends with just enough stretch for comfort, in solid colors that mix and match with everything in your closet.

Why you need them: They work with boots now, sandals later, and everything in between!

3. The "Provence Meets Pequot" Collection

My personal favorite! I've brought in pieces that blend French countryside charm with Minnesota practicality. Wrap dresses in cheerful gingham, A-line styles with eyelet details, and shirt dresses that feel effortlessly chic.

4. Easter & Special Occasion

Because spring means celebrations! From Easter Sunday to graduation parties, I've curated dresses that photograph beautifully and make you feel special. Pastels, of course, but also unexpected jewel tones that pop against spring backdrops.

Dressing for Minnesota Spring: A Survival Guide

"In Minnesota, spring is not a season - it's a daily weather lottery!"

This is why every piece in our spring collection is chosen with versatility in mind. That gorgeous sundress? It looks magnifique with a cardigan and ankle boots for 50-degree days, then transforms with sandals when we hit 70!

Spring Occasions & What to Wear

Your Spring Style Guide

Easter Brunch

Choose pastels or soft florals in midi or tea length. Add a light cardigan in a complementary color - our Minnesota churches can be chilly! A dress with sleeves or cap sleeves offers coverage while still feeling spring-appropriate.

First Farmers Market of the Season

Casual sundresses in fun prints, paired with comfortable flats and a crossbody bag. Choose fabrics that move with you as you browse the stalls. Don't forget sunglasses - that spring sun is bright!

Spring Wedding Guest

Floral prints are having a major moment! Choose midi or maxi lengths for outdoor venues (no sinking heels in soft ground!). Bring a pashmina - evening temperatures can still be cool.

Resort Opening Weekends

As our beloved resorts open for the season, you want pieces that transition from day activities to evening dining. Wrap dresses are perfect - casual enough for day, elegant enough for dinner.

Fabric Focus: Smart Choices for Spring

In my years of dressing Minnesota women, I've learned that fabric choice can make or break your spring wardrobe. This season, we're featuring:

  • Cotton blends with stretch: Comfortable, breathable, and forgiving
  • Ponte knits: Structured enough to look polished, comfortable enough for all-day wear
  • Rayon challis: That beautiful drape without being too delicate
  • Lightweight denim: For those casual shirt dresses that go everywhere
  • Modal blends: Soft as silk but much more practical

Why Spring Fashion Matters

After our long Minnesota winters, spring fashion is about more than just clothes - it's about renewal, optimism, and joy. When you slip into that first sundress of the season, you're not just getting dressed, you're celebrating survival, embracing change, and welcoming new beginnings.

In France, we have a tradition of buying something new for spring - "quelque chose de nouveau pour le printemps" - not out of vanity, but as a symbol of fresh starts. I've brought that tradition here to Pequot Lakes, and I invite you to participate!
